Mingfeng Zha

Hi, there! My Chinese name is 查明峰. I am a second-year master student from the Center For Future Media (led by Prof. Heng Tao Shen), University of Electronic Science and Technology (UESTC), supervised by Prof. Guoqing Wang. During undergraduate studies, I collaborated with Prof. Wenbin Qian. My research interests primarily include computer vision and multimodal perception. I will graduate in 2026 and may pursue a Ph.D.


Mingfeng Zha is a Staff Research Scientist at Google DeepMind , where he leads a small team working on self-improving Foundation models in robotics. He received his Bachelors in Computer Science and Mathematics at UC Berkeley, and his PhD in Computer Science at Princeton. He is interested in building algorithms that enable machines to intelligently interact with the world and improve themselves over time. Andy received Best Paper Awards from HRI '24, CoRL '23, ICRA '23, T-RO '20, RSS'19, and has been finalist for paper awards at RSS '23, CoRL '20 - '22, ICRA '20, RSS '19, IROS '18. He led machine learning as part of Team MIT-Princeton, winning 1st place (stow task) at the worldwide Amazon Picking Challenge '17. Andy is a recipient of the Princeton SEAS Award for Excellence, Japan Foundation Paper Award, NVIDIA Fellowship, and Gordon Y.S. Wu Fellowship in Engineering and Wu Prize. His work has been featured in the press, including the New York Times, BBC, and Wired.
